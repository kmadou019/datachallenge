QUESTION 1 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(5 MARKS) 

 

On 29 December 2011 you received a decision dated 27 December 2011 refusing your 

European patent application. 

The last sentence of the decision reads as follows: 

"As all the features of the independent claim are known from the prior art document D1, 

the application is refused for lack of novelty, Art. 56 EPC." 

On 26 January 2012 you received a correction of the decision according to 

Rule 140 EPC for the same application. The correction is dated 24 January 2012. Only 

the last sentence was changed to "As all the features of the independent claim are 

known from the prior art document D1, the application is refused for lack of novelty, 

Art. 54 EPC". 

 

Your client has not yet decided whether to file an appeal, but intends to take this 

decision in the next few weeks. However, he will call you this morning to discuss the 

further procedure. 

 

What will you advise your client?

QUESTION 2 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 (6 MARKS)  

 

For European patent application EP1, filed in English, the first communication from the 

examining division under Article 94(3) and Rule 71(1), (2) EPC dated 13 December 

2007 raised only a lack of clarity objection. On 14 December 2009 a divisional 

application DIV1 of EP1 was filed in the same language as EP1 at the EPO in Munich. 

On 30 September 2010 a further divisional application DIV2 of EP1 was filed in the 

same language as EP1 at the EPO in Berlin.  

The partial search report for DIV1 dated 6 October 2011 contained a statement 

regarding lack of unity. However, no additional search fee was paid. 

EP1 and DIV2 were withdrawn on 28 January 2011. 

 

a) What were the filing requirements for divisional applications DIV1 and DIV2? 

 

b) Is it still possible to validly file further divisional applications?

QUESTION 3 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(5 MARKS) 

 

On 5 October 2010 your Spanish client filed a PCT application in Spanish with the 

Spanish Patent and Trade Mark Office (SPTO). No priority was claimed. Due to a lack of 

unity objection, which the client accepts, only the first invention was searched by the 

SPTO. However, your client wants a search to be carried out by the EPO on the second 

invention before deciding on entry into the national/regional phase but does not want to 

file a new application. 

  

a) Is this possible?  

 

b) If so, what steps have to be taken?  

 

 

 

QUESTION 4 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(4 MARKS) 

 

Company X filed a draft of a scientific paper as a European patent application EP-X at 

the EPO. The draft describes compositions of the class A. The EPO accorded to 

application EP-X a date of filing of 20 December 2009. On 15 February 2010, 

company X filed, for EP-X, a claim directed to product A1, which was not described in 

the draft paper. 

On 17 November 2010 company X filed an international application PCT1 at the USPTO 

with the description of EP-X and the same claim as above, claiming priority from EP-X.  

Company X received from the USPTO an international search report indicating only a 

single document, which discloses product A1. This document was made available to the 

public in March 2010. 

 

a) Is it possible to obtain valid patent protection via the claim directed to product A1 

if PCT1 enters the European phase? 

 

b) How would the situation be different for PCT1 if the draft paper had disclosed A1?

Chinese State Intellectual Property Office (SIPO). In March 2011 the applicant received 

the international search report from SIPO. In May 2011 the applicant filed with the

amendments. The application will be published next week. 

 

a) Which parts of the publication will be in what language(s)?

Your client wants to use EP1 to obtain Europe-wide protection for both Inv1 and Inv2.  

 

What do you advise your client?

QUESTION 7 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(4 MARKS) 

 

Your client has developed the following way of diagnosing an illness: 

a) Carrying out a known method of biopsy by a medical practitioner to extract a liver 

tissue sample from a patient; 

b) carrying out a novel and inventive method for diagnosing the illness on the basis of 

the tissue sample in an analysing liquid. 

Your client has informed you that the way in which the tissue sample is obtained is not 

an essential feature of his development. 

 

Your client wants you to file a European patent application. Discuss the patentability of 

his development according to the EPC. 

 

 

 

QUESTION 8 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(5 MARKS) 

 

European patent application EP1 was published on 10 March 2010. In reply to a 

communication of the examining division, the applicant filed amended claims containing 

improved subject-matter in June 2011. This improved subject-matter is new and 

inventive over the originally disclosed subject-matter, and, in itself, is sufficiently 

disclosed according to Art. 83 EPC. On 9 December 2011 the applicant filed a second 

European patent application EP2 describing and claiming the improved subject-matter. 

 

Can the applicant obtain valid protection for the improved subject-matter via EP1 or 

EP2?
